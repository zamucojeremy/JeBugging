BigSpoon: Filipino Food Sharing Application

What is it about?

The team developed a web application that enables users to share their surplus food to provide nourishment for those in need. This approach fosters mutual assistance during challenging times, particularly in the wake of the recent pandemic, which disproportionately affected the less privileged. Drawing inspiration from community pantries, this project stands out by emphasizing a one-for-one food sharing concept. This unique feature motivates individuals with limited excess food to contribute, even if it's only to support a single person who requires it.
Within the scope of this project, I assumed the crucial dual role of a frontend designer and developer. My task encompassed crafting the visual and interactive aspects of the user interface. To achieve this, I harnessed the power of HTML and CSS, utilizing these foundational technologies to sculpt and breathe life into my design. Through meticulous coding and thoughtful design choices, I navigated the intricate dance between aesthetics and consistency, striving to create an inviting and seamless user experience... making sure that customers will see "food" as they see our website.

Struggles of Online Setup through the Pandemic

Navigating project teams in an online setup during the pandemic presented a unique set of challenges. The physical distance posed hurdles to the natural flow of communication and collaboration that thrive in face-to-face interactions. Miscommunications were more frequent, often stemming from the limitations of digital tools to capture nuances. The absence of real-time discussions hindered quick decision-making, sometimes leading to delays.

Nonetheless, these challenges served as catalysts for us to venture into inventive avenues of collaboration. We enthusiastically adopted virtual collaboration tools, honed our written communication proficiencies, and grew to value the adaptability inherent in an online arrangement. Despite the learning curve, our resolute determination and flexibility enabled us to surmount these obstacles. The outcome was a trove of valuable insights and enhanced capabilities in remote work.

Frontend design as my cup of tea

Diving into frontend development and design was a blast, quickly becoming my favorite. It brought back my earlier days as a kid who always wanted to create art, also blending creativity with technology. As I crafted layouts and interactions, it felt like painting with code. This fusion of art and tech was not only enjoyable but also fulfilling. Frontend work wasn't just a job; it was like rediscovering my inner artist in a new digital realm.
I enjoy spotting awesome website designs as a hobby. It's fun to find websites that look great and work well. I like figuring out what makes them special and learning about new design trends.
